As the last frost retreats and daylight stretches past six o'clock, farmers across southern Europe are turning their attention to one of the most consequential decisions of the year: what to plant, and when.

## Soil Temperature Is Everything

Agronomists agree that soil temperature — not calendar date — should drive planting decisions. For most vegetables, the magic number is 10°C at a depth of 5cm. Tomatoes, peppers, and aubergines prefer 15°C or warmer before transplanting.

"We've seen growers lose entire early plantings by rushing," says Dr. Nikos Stavros of the Hellenic Agricultural University. "A cold snap at germination does more damage than waiting an extra two weeks."

## What's Trending This Season

- **Drought-tolerant legumes** — chickpeas and lentils are seeing renewed interest as water costs rise
- **Heritage grains** — emmer wheat and einkorn are commanding premium prices at specialty markets
- **Cover crop mixes** — clover-and-vetch blends are becoming standard practice for soil health

## Timing Recommendations for the Eastern Mediterranean

| Crop | Sow Indoors | Transplant / Direct Sow |
|------|------------|------------------------|
| Tomatoes | Early March | Late April |
| Peppers | Mid-February | Early May |
| Courgettes | — | Mid-April |
| Basil | Late March | After last frost |
| Sunflowers | — | April–May |

## Looking Ahead

With forecasts pointing to a drier-than-average summer, experts recommend prioritizing soil organic matter now — through compost application and mulching — to retain moisture through the critical July–August period.

The window is open. Get planting.
